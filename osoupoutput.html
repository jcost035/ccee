Step One Register and enroll for an upcoming phase 1 certification training
Step Two Complete the Phase 1, Week-long Professional Development which includes:
Content themes of economic empowerment and equity
Student centric, standards-based and inquiry-driven (specified by the 1998 History-Social Science Standards, the 2016 CA History-Social Science Framework, and the National Council for Social Studies C3 Framework)
Expert presentations
Engaging simulations and activities (Not your dad’s economic course)
Course-planning time
Mentoring
Group and individual reflection
Debriefing and ongoing coaching
Step Three Complete Phase 2 which includes:
Applying the lessons from the Phase 1 training by building a portfolio
Making a contribution to economic education in the community (e.g., leading a workshop, promoting economic education at a school site through a student event, etc.)
Completing a comprehensive examination which includes a multiple choice test and free response essay questions.
Step Four Be recognized by both organizations, including a signed letter to your principal.

Then benefit from support of CCEE and CASET educators + fellow certification cohort teachers throughout your teaching career Once the above steps have been completed, you will be certified as a Superior California High School Economics Teacher
